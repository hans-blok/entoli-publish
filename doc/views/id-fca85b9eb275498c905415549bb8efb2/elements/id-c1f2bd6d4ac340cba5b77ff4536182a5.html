## Exploration **Exploration** is the phase in which an issue is opened, delimited and conceptually explored in order to identify relevant sources, concepts and hypotheses, without yet structuring or normatively recording them. Purpose: - Making the problem space explicit - Identifying relevant sources - Distinguishing relevant perspectives - Formulating initial hypotheses - Collecting and clarifying concepts - Controlled delimitation of the source space Sub-phases: 1. **Orientation** The issue is explored openly. Possible sources, angles and contexts are inventoried. New information may be identified. 2. **Selection** Relevant sources are explicitly chosen. The source space is delimited and recorded. Non-selected sources are excluded. 3. **Concept Inventory** Terms, definitions and assumptions are collected and provisionally clarified. No formal structuring or modelling yet. Characteristics: - Interpretation permitted - Exploration possible (in Orientation) - No formal structural enforcement yet - No normative status yet - Result is preparatory input for Ordering Interpretation Mode: Exploratory Source-regime: - Orientation: Open - Selection & Concept Inventory: Selection-bound [not a canonical Source-regime position — see alignment report]
