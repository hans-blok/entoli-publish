We apply Architectural Thinking ()
**Short description** Although AI is disruptive for the IT market, we continue to work according to existing architectural principles. ### Rationale Like earlier technological leaps (for example from machine language to higher-level programming languages), AI does not make IT simpler, but more complex. The fundamental challenges of software development — separation of concerns, maintainability, testability — remain and even become more important. This principle guards against falling into the trap of "magic" solutions where architecture is forgotten. AI is a powerful tool, but not a replacement for structural thinking. ### Implications **For system design**: - We continue to think in terms of concerns and responsibilities - Separation of concerns remains leading (also for AI components) - New technology changes tooling and implementation, not the architectural frame of thinking **For development**: - We avoid "magic" solutions without a clear architecture - Complexity is managed through structure and clarity, not cleverness - We explicitly document decisions and trade-offs **For governance**: - Architecture reviews remain mandatory, also for AI components - Existing quality gates and quality requirements remain in force - We apply proven patterns before introducing new ones ### Examples **Good** ✓ - Explicitly bounding and documenting agent capabilities in charters - Separating prompts from business logic and data - Clearly isolating state management and side effects - Designing agents according to the single responsibility principle **Bad** ✗ - Deploying a large AI model without a clear capability boundary - Mixing prompts with configuration, data and business rules in one file - "The AI will figure it out" without architectural consideration - Agents with vague, overlapping responsibilities and unclear boundaries
