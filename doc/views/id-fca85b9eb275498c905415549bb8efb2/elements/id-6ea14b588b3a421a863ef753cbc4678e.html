## Accountability **Accountability** is the phase in which the process, the choices made and the judgments reached are made explicit, transparent and traceable within the ecosystem. Purpose: - Giving insight into the steps followed and choices made - Safeguarding traceability to source artefacts - Making overviews and documentation available - Supporting transparency and governance oversight Sub-phases: 1. **Documenting** Relevant decisions, assumptions and considerations are explicitly recorded. 2. **Making Traceability Explicit** Relations between artefacts, norms and realisations are made insightful (e.g. matrices, traceability overviews). 3. **Contextualising** Positioning within the value stream, governance and mandate is clarified. 4. **Publishing & Sharing** Accountability information is made available to relevant stakeholders. Characteristics: - No new norm formation - No new structure formation - No new evaluation - Transparency about what has already taken place Interpretation Mode: Explanatory Source-regime: Canon-bound [See the UNMAPPED note on this element's name: this phase is not one of canon's six Development phase positions and is mis-numbered "5", colliding with Testing]
