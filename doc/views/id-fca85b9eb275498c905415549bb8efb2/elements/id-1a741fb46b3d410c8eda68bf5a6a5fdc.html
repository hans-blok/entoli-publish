# Operationalisation ## Definition **Operationalisation** is the phase in which a realised and verified structure is formally brought into effect within the intended context, thereby becoming operative reality. Where: - **Recording** makes meaning binding, - **Realisation** makes that meaning work, **Operationalisation** makes the working structure actually take effect in practice. Operationalisation marks the transition from: > working structure in the workspace > to > operative structure in reality. --- ## Characteristics - Context transition (from workspace to practice) - Formal or de facto activation - Change of operative reality - Can have legal, organisational or technical consequences - Is less reversible than realisation - Often has governance implications --- ## Positioning in the generic value stream 1. Exploration 2. Ordering 3. Recording 4. Realisation - Construction - Integration - Verification 5. Testing 6. Operationalisation Operationalisation thus follows after realisation and testing. see alignment report] --- # Sub-phases of Operationalisation A generic four-part model, applicable across software, governance, organisation, investment, etc. These four sub-phases correspond to the canonical Operationalisation sub-phases in entoli-concepts.md: Decision-making (Canon-bound), Activation (Input-bound), Guidance (Open), Consolidation (Canon-bound). --- ## 6.1 Decision-making ### Definition A formal or implicit decision that the realised structure may come into effect. ### Characteristics - Mandate or authority is exercised - Acceptance is ratified - Risk transition is acknowledged ### Examples **Software** - Go-live decision - Change approval **Non-software** - Formal ratification of new policy - Investment approval - Governance decision to introduce --- ## 6.2 Activation ### Definition Actually bringing the structure into effect, technically, organisationally or legally. ### Characteristics - Concrete transition to active status - Change of runtime or practice conditions - No longer hypothetical ### Examples **Software** - Deployment to production - Activating a feature flag - Making configuration live **Non-software** - Starting a new process - Rolling out an organisational structure - Making a contract take effect --- ## 6.3 Guidance ### Definition Guiding the transition from the old situation to the new one. ### Characteristics - Transition measures - Phase-out or migration - Stabilisation ### Examples **Software** - Data migration - Parallel run - Cutover **Non-software** - Handover period - Phased rollout - Onboarding those involved --- ## 6.4 Consolidation ### Definition Anchoring the new structure as regular, ongoing reality. ### Characteristics - Monitoring and stabilisation - Anchoring in policy or practice - Normalisation ### Examples **Software** - Monitoring in production - Incident management - Performance optimisation **Non-software** - Evaluation of the new process - Formal inclusion in governance - Structural budgeting
