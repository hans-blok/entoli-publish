## Recording **Recording** is the phase in which an ordered structure or behaviour description is converted into a normative, testable and formally anchored artefact within the ecosystem. Purpose: - Explicitly declaring the ordered structure as valid - Recording behaviour and definitions exactly and unambiguously - Safeguarding testability and traceability - Formally positioning the artefact within governance Sub-phases: 1. **Ratification & Formal Fixation** One chosen variant is normatively confirmed. Scope and mandate are made explicit. 2. **Formal Fixation** Structure and behaviour are formulated exactly and stably (e.g. logical data model, Gherkin). Terminology is frozen. 3. **Making Testability Explicit** Acceptance criteria, validation rules and traceability are recorded so that testing is possible. 4. **Publication & Anchoring** The artefact receives formal status (version, owner, positioning, validity) within the ecosystem. Characteristics: - No new structure or domain content - No further exploration or interpretation - Analysis turns into norm - The artefact becomes the reference point for realisation and testing Interpretation Mode: Normative Source-regime: Canon-bound
