## Ordering **Ordering** is the phase in which explored and selected input is converted into a coherent, consistent and structurally explicit structure suitable for normative recording. Purpose: - Reducing variation - Making implicit coherence explicit - Correctly positioning abstraction levels - Enforcing internal consistency - Preparing the structure for normative ratification Sub-phases: 1. **Conceptual Restructuring** Concepts, hypotheses or work items are abstracted, grouped and deduplicated. Variation is reduced and fundamental conceptual units are determined. 2. **Structure Formulation** Relations, hierarchy and dependencies are made explicit. For data: formal modelling (e.g. NIAM). For work: THEME → IMPROVEMENT → WORK-TASK structure. 3. **Reduction & Consistency Enforcement** Overlap is eliminated, redundancy removed and internal coherence checked. The structure is made minimal and coherent. Characteristics: - Interpretation within a closed source space is permitted - No new external content - No normative status - Iterative in nature Interpretation Mode: Structural Source-regime: Canon-bound
